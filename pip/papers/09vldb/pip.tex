\begin{abstract}
We describe PIP, a probabilistic database system that bridges powerful integration techniques with the evaluation of arbitrary queries over uncertain data.  PIP's approach encompasses and extends the efficiency of recent discrete systems such as MistiQ and MayBMS, as well as the generality of the Monte-Carlo approach taken by MCDB.  By maintaining a lossless symbolic representation of probabilistic data as it is transformed by queries, PIP is able to very efficiently compute expectations and other statistical metrics.

The additional state encoded in PIP's C-Tables style representation enables a wide variety of integration techniques.  This representation vastly broadens the range of algorithms available for computing expectations, makes it possible for PIP to select between various sampling and evaluation strategies, and reduces the amount of work required.  We show that even straightforward algorithms can make use of the added information to have a profound impact on the efficiency and accuracy of the computation of expectations, particularly in the case of highly selective queries - a class of problem that MCDB has trouble with.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}

We have shown that it is possible to apply the c-tables approach to probabilistic databases with only minimal overhead, even when it is used to represent arbitrary variable distributions.  The additional information provided by the c-table adds a degree of flexibility that allows PIP to outperform Sample-First approaches in a number of instances.
